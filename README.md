## Understanding Kratom: The Basics

**Kratom** (Mitragyna speciosa) is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia, where its leaves have been used traditionally for centuries. The active compounds, primarily **mitragynine** and **7-hydroxymitragynine**, interact with opioid receptors in the brain to produce various effects.

Modern processing methods have created two primary consumption forms:

- **Kratom powder**: Finely ground dried leaves
- **Kratom capsules**: Powder enclosed in gelatin or vegetarian capsules

Both forms contain identical alkaloids, but their delivery methods create distinct user experiences.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can kratom capsules be opened and consumed as powder?

Yes, kratom capsules can be opened and the contents consumed as powder. This allows users to experience faster onset times while maintaining the convenience of pre-measured doses. Simply twist apart the capsule halves and pour the powder into a liquid or consume using the toss-and-wash method.

### How many kratom capsules equal one teaspoon of powder?

Typically, **4-6 kratom capsules** equal approximately one teaspoon of powder, depending on capsule size and powder density. Most capsules contain 0.5-0.7 grams, while a level teaspoon holds roughly 2.5-3 grams of kratom powder. Always use a scale for precise measurements rather than relying on volume estimates.

### Do kratom capsules lose potency faster than powder?

Kratom capsules and powder have similar shelf lives when stored properly. The gelatin capsule actually provides additional protection against air and moisture exposure. However, capsules may become brittle over time, potentially affecting the encapsulated powder. Both forms maintain 85-95% potency for 1-2 years under optimal storage conditions.

### Is it safe to take kratom capsules with other supplements?

While kratom capsules are generally well-tolerated, interactions with other supplements or medications are possible. Avoid combining with sedating substances, blood thinners, or other botanicals affecting similar pathways. Always consult healthcare providers before mixing kratom with prescription medications or if you have underlying health conditions.

### Why do some people experience nausea with kratom capsules but not powder?

Nausea from kratom capsules may result from taking too many at once, overwhelming the stomach with concentrated powder upon dissolution. The gelatin itself can also cause stomach upset in sensitive individuals. Taking capsules with light food, spacing doses throughout the day, or switching to vegetarian capsules may help reduce nausea.

### Can kratom powder be made into homemade capsules?

Yes, kratom powder can be encapsulated at home using empty gelatin or vegetarian capsules and a capsule filling machine. This approach combines the cost savings of powder with the convenience of capsules. Size 00 capsules typically hold 0.5-0.7 grams of kratom powder, allowing for precise dosing while maintaining freshness.

### How long should someone wait between kratom capsule doses?

Wait at least **4-6 hours** between kratom capsule doses to avoid tolerance buildup and potential adverse effects. Since capsules have delayed onset, resist the urge to redose within the first hour. Most users find twice-daily dosing sufficient, with morning and evening doses spaced 8-12 hours apart for optimal effects and safety.
